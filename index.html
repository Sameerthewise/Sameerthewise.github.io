Brief History of the Internet

The internet began in the late 1960s as a project funded by the U.S. Department of Defense called ARPANET. ARPANET was designed to connect computers at various universities and research centers to share information quickly and reliably.

Early systems used packet switching, a method that breaks data into small pieces, or packets, which could travel independently across the network and then be reassembled at their destination. This approach was revolutionary compared to traditional circuit-switched networks.

Over time, ARPANET grew, more networks were connected, and protocols like TCP/IP were developed to standardize communication. This evolution allowed the internet to expand into a global network connecting billions of devices and enabling services like the World Wide Web, email, and more.
